% abstract 

\abstract{Electron ptychography has revolutionized atomic-scale imaging
by providing exceptional contrast for both light and heavy elements.
While current technology can achieve remarkable lateral resolution below
0.5 Ångstroms, the depth (axial) resolution has remained limited to
several nanometers---until now.

In this work, we introduce multi-slice ptychographic electron
tomography, a breakthrough technique that achieves true 3D
atomic-resolution imaging beyond conventional depth of field limits.
Using a Co₃O₄ nanocube as our test subject, we demonstrate:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Unprecedented 3D resolution: 2Å axial and 0.7Å transverse resolution
\item
  Large imaging volume: (18.2 nm)³
\item
  13.5× improvement in depth resolution compared to existing methods
\end{itemize}

This advancement significantly expands the capabilities of
high-resolution electron microscopy, opening new possibilities for
studying larger material volumes at atomic resolution.

Our ongoing research focuses on further refinements to enable 3D imaging
of individual weakly scattering atoms, promising even more detailed
insights into material structures.}
